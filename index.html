UI redesign of the United States Department of the Interior government website.

The United States Department of the Interior government website has a confusing and cluttered navigation, leaving users frustrated. Redesigning the website will improve UI elements and increase user accessibility.

Color Testing

The Department of the Interior website passes screen reader tests because contrast ratios are high enough between font and background colors.

Usability Testing

Our users are trying to find information on what the Department of Interior does (main programs, resources), will likely navigate to the about page first for a summary of mission and values.

Prototyping

Iterations
  • Outgoing link in primary navigation changed to different color
  • Add content side swipe animation to fully show scrolling feature
  • Differentiate which primary navigation tabs have drop down options

Final Thoughts

Hierarchy in primary navigation must be universally understood, otherwise alphabetical order is the easiest for new users. Icons must be universally understood, and button and tab components are incredibly useful for saving time and maintaining consistent design choices.The card sorting took up a lot of time for what felt like little result, as the majority of initial UI issues were due to a cluttered navigation, not the actual organization of subheaders. Additionally, an official government website might want more white space utilized, so further style iterations could be made in that direction
